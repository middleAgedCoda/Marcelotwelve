El Loco · Born May 12, 1988 · Rio de Janeiro · Brazil

From futsal
on the streets
of Rio to the Bernabéu
at 18.

His full name is Marcelo Vieira da Silva Júnior. He was born May 12, 1988 in Rio de Janeiro — into a neighbourhood where the street was the pitch. His mother was a teacher. His father was a firefighter. Marcelo nearly quit football due to financial hardship — until his grandfather, Pedro Vieira, stepped in. Pedro funded his training. Pedro took him to sessions every day. Pedro made Marcelo possible.

He started futsal at nine. Joined Fluminense's youth academy at 13. Made his professional debut in 2005, won a state title in his first season — and in January 2007, aged just 18, Real Madrid knocked on his door. "I came here. I can now say I am a madrileño. Here, I built a family with my wife." He would never stop moving — attacking full-back, winger, magician with the ball. El Loco. The crowd's favourite in any stadium on earth.

On February 6, 2025, Marcelo announced his retirement. His video ended with footage of him playing football with his two sons, Enzo and Liam. The legend ended where it began — with family.

"My love for football comes from my grandfather. He wanted me to be a professional footballer and did everything possible to make it happen."

Football Holding Company · Founded 2017
Group DOZE
DOZE means "Twelve" in Portuguese. Marcelo's football holding company — built while he was still playing for Real Madrid. The vision: own clubs, develop players, build the bridge between South American and European football. Three clubs and growing.
groupdoze.com →
🇧🇷
Azuriz FC · Marmeleiro, Paraná · Acquired 2017
Azuriz Futebol Clube
Campeonato Paranaense club. Majority shareholder. The Brazilian anchor in the DOZE portfolio — paired with Mafra to create a two-direction talent pipeline between Brazil and Portugal. Won Paraná's second division in 2020.
🇵🇹
CD Mafra · Lisbon Region, Portugal · Acquired 2021
Clube Desportivo de Mafra
Second division of the Portuguese Championship. The European anchor. The bridge between the Brazilian and European markets that Marcelo always envisioned. "Mafra, as a member of the same group as Azuriz, provides the ideal link."

Kings League Spain · October 2025 · Co-founder + Player
Skull FC
After retiring on February 6, 2025, Marcelo returned to competition in the Kings League Spain — co-founding Skull FC with Twitch streamer Plex and playing on the inaugural roster. He immediately delivered a Bernabéu-level dribble that lit up the internet. El Loco was back.

Marcelo's youth football development programme — built with his brother-in-law Caio Alves. An initiative to give young Brazilian players the pathway he never had easily. Campus 12, Academy 12 — the number follows him into everything.

Family · The Foundation · Clarice · Enzo · Liam · Pedro

The story always
begins and ends
with family.

❤️
Clarice, Enzo, Liam — and Pedro, always.
Marcelo married Clarice Alves in 2008. Their son Enzo Gattuso Alves Vieira was born September 24, 2009 — and by 2017, at just 8 years old, Enzo scored a hat-trick on his debut for Real Madrid's youth academy. Their second son, Liam Vieira, was born September 1, 2015.

His grandfather Pedro Vieira started everything. Pedro funded his training. Pedro took him every day. Pedro died in July 2014, during the World Cup in Brazil. Marcelo stayed — to honour his grandfather's wishes. A stadium at Fluminense now bears Pedro's name: the Marcelo Vieira Stadium.

The retirement video in February 2025 ended with footage of Marcelo playing football with Enzo and Liam. He arrived at Real Madrid at 18, alone and Brazilian. He left it as a Spaniard, a father, and the most decorated player in the club's history. He built a family in Madrid. Then he took that family home.
